So:

- ~85-90% of raw correlation is **structural shared market beta** (long-only × 54-stock universe × top-10 produces inherently high SPY/QQQ correlation; β_vs_SPY ranges 1.04-1.18 across candidates).

**Conclusion: the construction-collapse cause is not single-name concentration nor sector concentration. It is the structural shared-market-beta of any long-only top-N portfolio over a small (54-stock) universe.**

## What this cycle proves (and what it does not)

**Proven (by ≥ 200 trials × cap-aware construction × 78-symbol post-rebuild canonical panel)**:

1. The **construction-collapse failure mode is structural to long-only × top-N × small universe**, not to any specific factor pool or any specific concentration setting at the construction layer. Cluster_cap=0.20 + max_single=0.10 was a credible attempt to break it; it doesn't.
2. **NAV correlation between any two long-only top-10 portfolios over 54 mappable stocks is bounded below by ~0.85** when the portfolios are run on the same panel — the shared market beta accounts for ≈ 0.85 of the 0.90+ raw correlation. The remaining 0.10-0.15 of residual correlation matters for risk diversification but is below the 0.70 warn threshold for 95% of pairs.
3. The cycle #01/#02 mining sibling `beta_spy_60d × mom_12_1 × volume_ratio_20d` was a pre-rebuild data artifact — under post-rebuild canonical data it does not appear in top-10 trials.

**Not proven**:

1. Whether C-1 weekly cadence + cap-aware construction would meaningfully shift NAV correlation. (Cycle #02 refuted weekly + global top-N at the IC level; weekly + cap_aware not tested.)
2. Whether C-4 cross-asset (cash sleeve / bonds / commodities) breaks correlation by enlarging the eligible universe.
3. Whether long-short (C-2) breaks it. Constraint says no — invariant is long-only.
4. Whether a defensive low-beta candidate + the existing pair gives a true 3-candidate fleet with diversification at the residual layer. (Concerns A/B at fleet stage.)
